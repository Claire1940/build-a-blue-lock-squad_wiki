<Section icon={Target} title="The Training Cycle: From G-Rank to SS-Rank">

One of the most confusing aspects for newcomers trying to **build a blue lock squad** is why their newly pulled characters cannot be added to the team immediately. In this game, characters are "locked" in a G-rank state until they undergo a Training session. Training is the process where you define a character's stats and "finalize" their version for use in actual matches.

- Detailed walkthrough of the 24-turn training system and stat allocation.
- How to navigate the training matches to ensure a high-rank finish.
- Explaining the difference between character level and training rank.

<YouTubeEmbed videoId="xuKpcrILeyE" title="[BEGINNER GUIDE] THE BEST WAY TO START & PROGRESS" />

The training process involves a 24-turn countdown. Each action you take consumes a turn and increases specific stats like Speed, Power, or Technique. Your goal is to reach zero turns while maximizing the character's Battle Power (BP).

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select Your Trainee">
    Choose the 3-star character you want to activate. Ensure you have enough stamina before starting, as exiting mid-session may result in sub-optimal stat gains.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Navigate the 24-Turn Grid">
    Click on training options to decrease the turn counter from 24 to 0. Focus on "Extreme" or "Best" rated training sessions to get the highest stat multipliers.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Win Training Matches">
    Periodically, you will be forced into matches. Winning these is crucial as they provide massive BP boosts and determine whether you jump from G-rank to D, C, or even B-rank in a single session.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Finalize and Compose">
    Once the turns hit zero, the character's rank is locked (e.g., D+ rank). You can now officially add this specific "trained version" of the character to your squad.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Stat Balancing Tip">
While it is tempting to go all-in on Speed, a balanced build is often better for beginners. Aim for a "Good" or "Extreme" rating on muscle training and tactical drills to ensure your player doesn't get bullied off the ball in higher-level matches.
</Callout>
