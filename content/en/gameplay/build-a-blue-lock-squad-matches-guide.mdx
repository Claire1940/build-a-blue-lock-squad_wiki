<Section icon={Target} title="Optimizing Formations and Positional Links">

Formations are the backbone of your strategy. A common mistake players make is placing high-OVR players in the wrong positions just to increase the team's average. This breaks chemistry links, which are visualized as blue lines between players. If these lines turn yellow or disappear, your team will perform significantly worse during matches, regardless of their individual ratings.

The most effective formations in the current 2026 meta are listed below. Choosing the right one depends on whether you have elite wingers or if you prefer to attack through the center.

| Formation | Best For | Key Weakness |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **4-3-3** | Wing-play and crossing | Vulnerable to counter-attacks |
| **3-4-3** | Maximum offensive pressure | Requires elite Center Backs |
| **4-4-2** | Defensive stability | Can lack creative passing |
| **4-5-1** | Midfield dominance | Striker can become isolated |

**Positional Priority for Maximum Chemistry:**
- **Goalkeeper (GK):** 1 player
- **Defenders (LB, RB, CB):** 4 players
- **Midfielders (LM, RM, CM):** 3 players
- **Forwards (LW, RW, ST):** 3 players

Some rare players have "Flex Positions," allowing them to maintain 100% chemistry in two or even three different spots. These players are the "jokers" of your squad and should be prioritized during rerolls.
